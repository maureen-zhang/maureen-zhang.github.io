== Final Project ==

The final project should be completed in groups of 2-3 students. It is a great opportunity to apply the techniques you have learned in class to a real-world dataset.

- Project objectives -

  • Prepare your selected dataset.
  • Apply appropriate supervised learning techniques.
  • Optionally, apply advanced techniques such as Deep Learning or Decision Trees.
  • Perform model evaluation and interpret the results.
  • Present your findings in a clear and organized report.

- Dataset selection -

Choose a dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository.

Some recommended datasets to consider:

  • Iris
  • Wine
  • Adult (Census Income)
  • Heart Disease
  • Bank Marketing

- Model selection -

  • Linear Regression and Polynomial Regression.
  • Classification Techniques: Naive Bayes, Logistic Regression, LDA, QDA, SVM.
  • Resampling Methods: Cross-Validation, Bootstrapping.
  • Deep Learning.
  • Decision Trees and Random Forests.
  • Unsupervised Learning: PCA and Clustering.

- Final Project Report guidelines -

  1. Preparing your data: Perform exploratory data analysis, identify goal/target features to predict, and preprocess the data as needed.
  2. Model selection: Apply at least two models (e.g., Linear Regression, Logistic Regression, or Decision Trees) and justify your choices.
  3. Evaluation: Use metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, MSE, or R-squared to evaluate your models.
  4. Analysis: Summarize your findings, compare the performance of your models, and provide insights.
  5. Report: Submit a well-documented Jupyter Notebook following PEP 8 Style guidelines.

- AI -

  • You are encouraged to use AI creatively. Please explain how and why AI was utilized in your project.
  • The final project must not be entirely written by AI.

- Deliverables -

  • A Jupyter Notebook containing your code, analysis, and results.
  • A presentation of less than 10 minutes delivered during the Lab.

- Deadlines -

The presentation is due on March 11th. The report is due in the last week of class. Specific submission details will be announced closer to the deadline.

  • 50% Project Report.
